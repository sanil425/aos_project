The Breath Behind the Game

Where breath meets movement. Slow moments, real sound, athlete reflections.

About the project: We’re collecting slow motion clips of impact and release across sports, paired with athletes’ words and breathing. Each sport has one or two pieces. Swipe between them and listen for the rhythm.

Tennis

Swipe • 2 pieces

Tennis Serve
This video explores how breath moves with the body through a tennis serve. The inhale lifts and prepares the motion, while the exhale releases power at contact. Breathing shapes the rhythm of the serve, turning force into something controlled and fluid.

Athlete: “If I don’t breathe right on the toss, everything feels rushed. One clean inhale and it settles.”
Piece 1
Inhale on the toss. Hold for a beat. Exhale through contact.
Piece 2
Second serve rhythm. Smaller pause. Longer exhale through the follow through.

Cricket

Swipe • 2 pieces

Cricket Shot
This clip emphasizes the poise and stillness of the batter before the shot is played. In that quiet moment, steady breathing centers the body and sharpens focus. As the bat moves, breath supports balance and control, linking calm preparation to decisive action.

Athlete: “I’m trying to be calm before it comes. If my breathing is steady, my feet feel steady.”
Piece 1
Stillness first. Then everything moves at once.
Piece 2
Breath holds you steady. The swing is just the release.

Football

1 piece

Football Kick
This video highlights how breath and preparation shape a football kick. The planted foot beside the ball creates stability, while a controlled exhale at impact channels power through the strike. Breath carries the motion from stillness into force and follow-through.

Athlete: “Plant. Stay loose. Breathe out on impact. If I hold my breath, the kick feels stiff.”
Piece 1
Plant foot. Quiet breath. Exhale on impact.
